BIRYANI
01 INTRO 02 SPICES REQUIRED 03 START COOKING

A LEGENDARY TASTE

For 499 years, the aroma of Biryani has travelled through royal kitchens, bustling streets, and warm homes — a timeless dish that tells a story in every grain.

THE SPICES

2 bay leaves, 1–2 cinnamon sticks, 4–5 cloves, 3 green cardamoms, 1 black cardamom, 1 star anise, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 6–8 black peppercorns, small mace, pinch of stone flower (kalpasi), 1 tsp turmeric, 1.5 tsp red chili, 2 tsp coriander, 1.5 tsp garam masala, 2 tbsp ghee, fried onions, fresh mint & coriander, lemon juice, rose/kewra water.

Cooking Steps

Step 1

Soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Let it rest for at least 1 hour.

Step 2

Heat ghee and oil in a pan. Sauté sliced onions until golden brown. Add whole spices like bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. Stir in the marinated chicken and cook until oil separates.

Step 3

Boil soaked rice with salt and a few drops of lemon juice until it's 70% cooked. Drain and set aside. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken, adding fried onions, saffron milk, mint, and coriander leaves.

Step 4

Cover the pot with a tight lid or dough seal. Cook on a low flame (dum) for 25–30 minutes. Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening. Serve hot with raita or salan.
