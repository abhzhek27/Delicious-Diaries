STRAWBERRY
01 INTRO 02 INGREDIENTS 03 MAKING STEPS

Sweet, Creamy, and Bursting with Berry Flavor

Strawberry ice cream is a classic frozen dessert, beloved for its vibrant color, refreshing taste, and delightful chunks of real strawberries. It's perfect for a warm day or any sweet craving!

INGREDIENTS REQUIRED

Fresh Strawberries – 2 cups (hulled and chopped)
Granulated Sugar – ½ to ¾ cup (adjust to taste and strawberry sweetness)
Heavy Cream (or Double Cream) – 1½ cups
Whole Milk – ½ cup
Vanilla Extract – 1 tsp
Lemon Juice – 1 tbsp (optional, brightens strawberry flavor)
Pinch of Salt

Making Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Strawberries

In a medium bowl, combine the chopped strawberries with about half of the granulated sugar. Let them sit for 15-30 minutes to macerate, releasing their juices. You can lightly mash them with a fork for a smoother texture, or leave them chunky.

Step 2: Create the Ice Cream Base

In a large bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, whole milk, remaining granulated sugar (start with less and add more to taste), vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. If using, add the lemon juice here too. Whisk until the sugar is dissolved.

Step 3: Combine and Chill

Gently stir the macerated strawberries (including their juices) into the cream mixture. Cover the bowl and chill the entire mixture in the refrigerator for at least 2-4 hours, or preferably overnight. This is crucial for a good texture.

Step 4: Churn and Freeze

Pour the chilled strawberry mixture into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions (usually 20-30 minutes), until it reaches a soft-serve consistency. Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze for at least 4-6 hours until firm. If you don't have an ice cream maker, you can use a no-churn method by whipping cold heavy cream to stiff peaks and folding it into a strawberry-sweetened condensed milk mixture, then freezing.

Step 5: Enjoy!

Scoop and enjoy your homemade strawberry ice cream! For best results, let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes before serving if it's very hard from the freezer.
