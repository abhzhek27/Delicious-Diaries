BEEF ULARTHIYATHU
01 INTRO 02 SPICES REQUIRED 03 START COOKING

THE PRIDE OF KERALA

Beef Ularthiyathu — the slow-roasted, spice-rich delicacy of Kerala — is more than just a dish. With crispy coconut slices, caramelized onions, and bold black pepper, it’s a celebration of tradition, flavor, and festive joy in every bite.

THE SPICES

1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp coriander powder, 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 tsp fennel seeds, 2 cinnamon sticks, 4 cloves, 2 cardamoms, fresh curry leaves, 2 onions sliced, 1/2 cup coconut bits, salt to taste, and coconut oil for roasting.

Cooking Steps

Step 1

Marinate beef with turmeric, chili powder, coriander, salt, and ginger-garlic paste. Pressure cook with a little water until tender. Reserve the stock.

Step 2

Heat coconut oil in a thick-bottomed pan. Add coconut bits and fry until golden. Remove and keep aside.

Step 3

In the same oil, sauté whole spices and curry leaves. Add onions and cook till browned and caramelized.

Step 4

Add cooked beef along with some reserved stock. Stir and roast on low heat, stirring frequently, until the gravy thickens and coats the meat.

Step 5

Finish with pepper, garam masala, fried coconut bits, and more curry leaves. Serve hot with Kerala parotta, rice, or tapioca.
