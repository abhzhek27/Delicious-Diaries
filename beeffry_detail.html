BEEF FRY
01 INTRO 02 SPICES REQUIRED 03 START COOKING

KERALA'S BLACK GOLD

Beef Fry, or “Beef Ularthiyathu”, is a beloved Kerala delicacy known for its bold flavors and fiery tempering. Cooked slow with whole spices and coconut slices, this dry roast dish is rich, rustic, and full of character — best served with Malabar parotta or rice.

THE SPICES

1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp crushed black pepper, 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tbsp coriander powder, 1 tsp fennel powder, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 2 bay leaves, 4–5 cloves, 2 cinnamon sticks, curry leaves, coconut oil, and sliced coconut bits for roasting.

Cooking Steps

Step 1

Marinate beef pieces with turmeric, salt, chili powder, coriander powder, and ginger-garlic paste. Pressure cook until tender with a little water.

Step 2

In a large pan, heat coconut oil. Fry coconut slices until golden and crisp. Set aside for garnish.

Step 3

In the same pan, add bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, and curry leaves. Sauté until fragrant.

Step 4

Add cooked beef and its stock. Roast on medium flame, stirring continuously until all moisture evaporates and beef turns deep brown.

Step 5

Sprinkle garam masala and black pepper. Add fried coconut slices and give it one last stir. Serve hot with parotta or steamed rice.
