BUTTERSCOTCH
01 INTRO 02 INGREDIENTS 03 MAKING STEPS

Rich, Buttery, and Caramel-Kissed Sweetness

Butterscotch ice cream is a luxurious treat, known for its deep golden color and distinctive caramelized brown sugar and butter flavor. It's a comforting dessert that truly melts in your mouth.

INGREDIENTS REQUIRED

**For the Butterscotch Sauce:**
Unsalted Butter – ½ cup (113g)
Light Brown Sugar – 1 cup (200g)
Heavy Cream – ¼ cup (60ml)
Pinch of Salt

**For the Ice Cream Base:**
Heavy Cream – 1½ cups (360ml)
Whole Milk – 1 cup (240ml)
Granulated Sugar – ¼ cup (50g, adjust based on sweetness preference)
Large Egg Yolks – 3-4 (optional, for richer custard)
Pure Vanilla Extract – 1 teaspoon

Making Steps

Step 1: Make the Butterscotch Sauce

In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the brown sugar and stir constantly until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is bubbly. Remove from heat and carefully stir in the ¼ cup heavy cream and pinch of salt. Mix until smooth. Let the butterscotch sauce cool down significantly, preferably to room temperature.

Step 2: Prepare the Cream Base

In a separate medium saucepan, combine the 1½ cups heavy cream, whole milk, and granulated sugar. Heat over medium heat until hot and steamy, but not boiling. If using egg yolks (for a custard base), whisk them in a bowl. Gradually whisk about half of the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks to temper them, then pour the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until the base thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Strain if desired.

Step 3: Combine and Chill

Stir the cooled butterscotch sauce and vanilla extract into the prepared cream base. Mix thoroughly until well combined. Cover the mixture with plastic wrap, pressing it directly onto the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate the mixture until it is completely chilled, at least 4 hours or preferably overnight.

Step 4: Churn and Freeze

Pour the thoroughly chilled butterscotch ice cream base into your ice cream maker. Churn according to the manufacturer's instructions, typically for 20-30 minutes, until it reaches a soft-serve consistency. Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container. Cover tightly and freeze for at least 4-6 hours, or until firm. Enjoy your rich, homemade butterscotch ice cream!
