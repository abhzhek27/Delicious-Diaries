CHOCOLATE ICECREAM
01 INTRO 02 INGREDIENTS 03 INSTRUCTIONS

Indulge in the richness of chocolate!

Chocolate Icecream is a classic dessert enjoyed by people all over the world for its deep, rich flavor and soft, moist texture.

INGREDIENTS

Heavy Cream : This is essential for the rich, creamy texture of ice cream.
Milk: Whole milk is typically recommended for its fat content, contributing to creaminess.
Cocoa Powder: Unsweetened cocoa powder provides the primary chocolate flavor. Dutch-process cocoa powder can give a darker color and richer, less bitter chocolate taste.
Sugar: Granulated sugar is used for sweetness. Some recipes may also use brown sugar for a slight caramel note.
Vanilla Extract: Enhances the chocolate flavor and adds complexity.
Salt: A pinch of salt balances the sweetness and makes the chocolate flavor pop.

Baking Steps

Step 1

Make the Chocolate Base
In a medium bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, unsweetened cocoa powder, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt until smooth and well combined. This is your concentrated chocolate flavor base.

Step 2

Whip the Cream.
In a large, chilled mixing bowl, pour in the cold heavy cream. Using an electric mixer, beat the cream on medium-high speed until it forms stiff peaks. This means the cream will hold its shape when you lift the beaters. Be careful not to over-whip, or it will become lumpy.

Step 3

Combine and Fold.
Gently pour the chocolate base from Step 1 into the whipped cream from Step 2. Using a spatula, carefully fold the chocolate mixture into the whipped cream. Do this in a gentle, "cut-and-fold" motion, scraping the bottom of the bowl and bringing it over the top, until no white streaks of cream remain. Be careful not to deflate the whipped cream.

Step 4

Freeze. Pour the mixture into your freezer-safe container. Smooth the top with a spatula. Cover the container tightly with a lid or plastic wrap (pressing the plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the ice cream can help prevent ice crystals). Freeze for at least 6-8 hours, or preferably overnight, until firm.
