BEEF BIRIYANI
01 INTRO 02 SPICES REQUIRED 03 START COOKING

A FEAST OF DEPTH & FLAVOR

Beef Biryani is a bold and satisfying dish that embodies the richness of spices and slow-cooked meat. It's a celebration of tradition and taste, bringing together tender beef, fragrant rice, and layers of aroma in every bite.

THE SPICES

2 bay leaves, 1–2 cinnamon sticks, 4–5 cloves, 3 cardamoms, 1 star anise, 1 tsp fennel seeds, 1/2 tsp peppercorns, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp red chili powder, 1 tbsp coriander powder, 1.5 tsp garam masala, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, fried onions, fresh coriander & mint leaves, lemon juice, and ghee or coconut oil.

Cooking Steps

Step 1

Marinate beef with turmeric, red chili, salt, and ginger-garlic paste. Pressure cook until tender. Set aside the cooked beef and reserve the stock.

Step 2

In a pan, sauté sliced onions until golden brown. Add whole spices and ground masalas. Stir in the cooked beef and mix well.

Step 3

Cook basmati rice separately with salt, whole spices, and lemon juice until 80% done. Drain and set aside.

Step 4

Layer beef masala and rice in a thick-bottomed pan. Sprinkle fried onions, mint, coriander, saffron water, and ghee between layers.

Step 5

Seal the pot and cook on low flame (dum) for 20–25 minutes. Let it rest for 10 mins before serving. Fluff and serve hot with raita or salad.
